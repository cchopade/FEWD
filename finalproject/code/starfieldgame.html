Welcome to Kepler 452b

452b is a rocky world orbiting a sun-like star at almost the same distance that Earth orbits its sun

Fun Fact: Due to its similarities to earth, 452b is dubbed "Earth 2.0"

Welcome to KIC 8462852

This star's light ocassionally dims by as much as 20%, suggesting that there is some material in orbit around the star that blocks its light

Fun Fact: Possible explanations for the variance range from an alien megastructure harnessing energy from the sun (like a Dyson Sphere) to a swarm of comets that got stuck in orbit

Welcome to Andromeda

Andromeda is a spiral galaxy and the nearest major galaxy to our own Milky Way

Fun Fact: Andromeda has one trillion stars

Welcome to Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet and a member of the Kuiper Belt, a mass of objects that orbit the sun beyond Neptune

Fun Fact: NASA's New Horizons spacecraft flew past Pluto in July 2015

Welcome to HD189773 Ab

The planet's blue color is due to the presence of magnesium silicate micro crystals that scatter the light of its star

Fun Fact: The planet is constantly being swept by a horizontal rain of hot sand

Welcome to Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in the Solar System

Fun Fact: Scientists at NASA recently discovered evidence of liquid water on Mars' surface

Welcome to Comet 67P

67P is the destination of the European Space Agency's Rosetta mission. Rosetta's lander, Philae, landed on 67P in November 2014, becoming the first spacecraft to land on a comet

Fun Fact: Each region of the comet is named after an egyptian deity

Welcome to Europa

Europa is one of Jupiter's moons and the sixth largest in the solar system

Fun Fact: Scientists hypothesize that there is a very active ocean below Europa's frozen surface, making it a candidate for life forms
